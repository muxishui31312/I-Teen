About Us

The I-Teen humanoid soccer robot team was formed by separating some members from the I-Kid team to participate in the RoboCup 2026 Middle Division international competition. Relying on the distinctive disciplines of the School of Instrument Science and Optoelectronic Engineering, the team actively explores aspects such as humanoid soccer robot structural design, machine vision, gait planning, and behavioral decision-making, continuously upgrading and optimizing the hardware platform and theoretical algorithms. The team recruits new members from graduate and undergraduate students every year. Since its establishment in 2010, the team has cultivated a large number of professionals, significantly improving students' practical and innovative abilities, and serving as an important platform for cultivating outstanding talents at the university. The team has achieved remarkable competitive results, such as winning the championship at the 2024 RoboCup Asia-Pacific Robotics Tianjin International Invitational Tournament, repeatedly winning awards in the humanoid soccer category and technical challenge championships at the RoboCup World Championships China from 2021 to 2025, and achieving a top-8 finish in the 3V3 soccer competition at the 2025 Robotics World Games.
